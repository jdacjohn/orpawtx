TSTC West Texas Institutional Effectiveness & Information Research

Student Portfolios

The Student Portfolios page is the gateway to IEIR's online system where instructors can enter and then view essential skills data for their program. Each program offered at TSTC West Texas has identified certain skills essential to success in the workplace. Instructors assess the level of skills students possess upon entry in the program, at an interim point, and during each student's capstone experience prior to graduation. In the "Rubrics" section, instructors post entry, interim, and capstone assessments of specific essential skills demonstrated by each student using rubric forms developed by the Student Enhancement Project (SEP) Team. Results of two other types of assessment are also collected in the Student Portfolios area; the Test of Everyday Reasoning, a test of critical thinking, and the Accuplacer tests of reading and writing.

From the Student Portfolios page, instructors can view the distribution of the level of skills in the program. In addition, faculty can determine the progress of students, individually and as a group from enrollment to graduation, and the degree of success in teaching essential skills over a period of time.
